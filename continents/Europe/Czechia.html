Geography

Heart of Central Europe

Czechia, also known as the Czech Republic, is a landlocked country bordered by Germany, Poland, Austria, and Slovakia. Its position makes it a major crossroads of European trade and culture.

Mountain Borders and Rivers

The country is surrounded by mountain ranges such as the Sudetes and the Bohemian Forest, while major rivers like the Vltava and Elbe flow through its territory.

History

Bohemian Kingdom

In the Middle Ages, the Kingdom of Bohemia was a powerful regional force and part of the Holy Roman Empire. Prague became one of Europe’s most important cultural centers.

Habsburg Rule and National Awakening

For centuries, Czech lands were under Habsburg control. The 19th century saw a rise in Czech national identity and cultural revival.

Velvet Revolution and Modern Era

After being part of Czechoslovakia for most of the 20th century, Czechia gained peaceful independence in 1993 during the Velvet Divorce with Slovakia.

Government

Parliamentary Republic

Czechia is a parliamentary democracy with a President as head of state and a Prime Minister leading the government. It is a stable member of the European Union and NATO.

Population

Urban-Centric Population

The country has about 10.8 million people, with Prague being the largest city and cultural hub. Other major cities include Brno, Ostrava, and Plzeň.

Highly Developed Living Standards

Czechia boasts high-quality healthcare, education, and one of the lowest unemployment rates in the EU.

Culture

Literary and Musical Heritage

Czechia has produced famous composers like Antonín Dvořák and writers such as Franz Kafka. The country has a strong tradition of classical music and theater.

Festivals and Traditions

Traditional festivals such as Masopust (Czech Carnival) and Easter celebrations featuring painted eggs are an important part of Czech culture.

Economy

Industrial and Innovation Hub

Czechia has a strong industrial base, especially in automobiles, manufacturing, and technology. Škoda Auto is one of the country’s most famous brands.

Strong Export Economy

The country is one of the largest exporters in Central Europe, with Germany being its biggest trading partner.

Cuisine

Hearty Central European Dishes

Traditional Czech cuisine includes dishes like roast pork with dumplings and sauerkraut, goulash, and fried cheese (smažený sýr).

Beer Culture

Czechs are famous for their beer, consuming the most per capita in the world. Pilsner-style beer originated here in the city of Plzeň.

Wildlife

Forests and National Parks

Czechia is rich in protected nature areas such as Šumava and Krkonoše National Parks. These forests are home to deer, lynx, and diverse bird species.

Tourism

Prague — The City of a Hundred Spires

Prague is one of Europe’s most beautiful cities, famous for its medieval Old Town, Charles Bridge, and Prague Castle.

Spa Towns and Castles

Cities like Karlovy Vary and Mariánské Lázně are world-renowned spa destinations, while Český Krumlov is a UNESCO-listed fairytale town.

Fun Facts

World’s Most Beer-Loving Country

Czechs drink more beer per person than any other nation in the world — and proudly consider it part of their cultural heritage.

Home of the Word “Robot”

The term “robot” was coined by Czech writer Karel Čapek in his 1920 play “R.U.R.” — and is now used worldwide.
