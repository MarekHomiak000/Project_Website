Geography

Peninsula, Islands, and Mountains

Spain occupies most of the Iberian Peninsula and includes the Balearic Islands in the Mediterranean, the Canary Islands in the Atlantic, and two enclaves in North Africa — Ceuta and Melilla.

Diverse Climate Zones

The climate varies from Mediterranean along the coast, to continental in the interior, and even oceanic in the green northern regions like Galicia and Asturias.

History

Roman, Islamic, and Christian Eras

Spain was part of the Roman Empire, later flourished under Islamic rule in Al-Andalus, and eventually unified under Christian kingdoms during the Reconquista.

Global Empire and Modern Transition

Spain became one of the world’s first global empires in the 15th century, declined in later centuries, and transitioned to democracy after Franco’s dictatorship ended in 1975.

Government

Parliamentary Monarchy

Spain is a parliamentary constitutional monarchy, with King Felipe VI as head of state and an elected Prime Minister as head of government.

Population

Diverse and Urban

Spain has around 48 million people, with major cities including Madrid, Barcelona, Valencia, and Seville. It is home to vibrant regional identities such as Catalan, Basque, and Andalusian.

Culture

Art, Architecture, and Music

Spain is known for Gaudí’s surreal architecture in Barcelona, flamenco music and dance from Andalusia, and world-famous artists like Picasso, Dalí, and Goya.

Festivals and Traditions

Iconic events include La Tomatina, Running of the Bulls in Pamplona, and vibrant Semana Santa (Holy Week) processions across the country.

Economy

Tourism and Services

Spain is one of the most visited countries in the world, with tourism contributing heavily to its economy, alongside strong sectors in agriculture, fashion, and renewable energy.

Wine and Agriculture

It is a global leader in olive oil and wine production — with famous wine regions like Rioja, Ribera del Duero, and Priorat.

Cuisine

Regional Specialties

Spanish cuisine is known for dishes like paella from Valencia, tapas culture, Basque pintxos, and Iberian ham — considered one of the finest cured meats in the world.

Healthy Mediterranean Diet

Spain’s diet is based on fresh seafood, olive oil, vegetables, and wine — part of the UNESCO-recognized Mediterranean diet.

Wildlife

Unique Iberian Ecosystems

Spain is home to the rare Iberian lynx, Iberian wolf, and large bird populations such as vultures and eagles living in mountain and forest habitats.

Protected Natural Parks

Doñana and Picos de Europa National Parks protect wetlands, forests, and endangered species, making Spain a biodiversity hotspot in Europe.

Tourism

Coastal Paradises and Cities

The Costa Brava, Costa del Sol, and Balearic Islands attract millions of tourists annually, alongside world-class cities like Barcelona and Madrid.

Historical and Cultural Routes

Landmarks like the Alhambra in Granada, Sagrada Familia in Barcelona, and the Camino de Santiago pilgrimage route offer rich cultural experiences.

Fun Facts

World Leader in UNESCO Sites

Spain is among the top countries globally with the most UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including historic cities, cathedrals, and unique landscapes.

Siesta Tradition

Spain is famous for the “siesta” — a traditional midday rest — although it is less common in large modern cities today.
