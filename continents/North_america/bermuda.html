Geography

Atlantic Archipelago

Bermuda is a British Overseas Territory located in the North Atlantic Ocean. It consists of approximately 138 islands, with the seven largest connected by bridges and causeways forming the main inhabited area.

Hills, Coves, and Pink Sand Beaches

The islands feature limestone hills, narrow inlets, and famous pink sand beaches. The climate is subtropical, with mild winters and warm summers moderated by ocean currents.

History

Discovery and Settlement

Bermuda was discovered in 1503 by Spanish explorers but was first settled by the English in 1609 after the shipwreck of the Sea Venture. It became a key colony in the Atlantic trade routes.

Colonial Era

Over the centuries, Bermuda developed as a strategic naval base for Britain, especially during the American Revolutionary War and both World Wars.

Government

British Overseas Territory

Bermuda is a self-governing British Overseas Territory. It has a parliamentary system with a Governor representing the British monarch, while the Premier leads the elected government.

Population

Small and Diverse

Bermuda has a population of around 65,000, consisting mainly of Bermudians of European and African descent. English is the official language, and the population enjoys a high standard of living.

Culture

Blend of Traditions

Bermuda’s culture reflects British heritage mixed with African and Caribbean influences. Festivals, music, and art celebrate this cultural blend.

Sports and Arts

Sailing, cricket, and golf are popular sports, while local artists, museums, and performing arts showcase the island’s history and traditions.

Economy

Finance and Tourism

Bermuda’s economy relies heavily on international finance, insurance, and reinsurance sectors. Tourism also contributes, with visitors drawn to beaches, history, and marine activities.

Cuisine

Seafood Staples

Bermudian cuisine emphasizes seafood such as codfish, lobster, and fish chowder. Dishes are often influenced by British culinary traditions.

International Flavors

Modern Bermudian food includes a fusion of Caribbean, Portuguese, and American flavors, especially in urban restaurants and resorts.

Wildlife

Birds and Marine Life

Bermuda hosts native and migratory birds, including the Bermuda petrel, or Cahow, rediscovered in 1951. Surrounding waters support tropical fish, sea turtles, and coral reefs.

Tourism

Beaches and Water Sports

Tourists visit Bermuda for its pink sand beaches, snorkeling, diving, and sailing opportunities. Historical sites like forts and colonial architecture attract visitors as well.

Fun Facts

Pink Sand Beaches

Bermuda is famous for its pink sand beaches, a result of crushed coral mixing with white sand.

Cahow Rediscovery

The Bermuda petrel, once thought extinct, was rediscovered in 1951, becoming a symbol of conservation success.
