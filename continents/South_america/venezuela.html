Geography

Coastal and Mountainous Landscapes

Venezuela is located in northern South America, bordered by the Caribbean Sea. Its geography includes the Andes mountains, vast plains (llanos), the Orinoco River basin, and tropical forests.

Angel Falls and Natural Wonders

Venezuela is home to Angel Falls, the world’s tallest waterfall, and other natural attractions such as Canaima National Park, tepuis (tabletop mountains), and the Llanos wetlands.

History

Indigenous Peoples

Before European arrival, Venezuela was inhabited by diverse Indigenous groups like the Timoto-Cuica, Caribs, and Arawaks.

Spanish Colonization

Spain colonized Venezuela in the 16th century, exploiting its resources and establishing settlements such as Caracas.

Independence

Venezuela gained independence from Spain in 1821 under Simón Bolívar, becoming part of Gran Colombia before establishing itself as a republic in 1830.

Government

Federal Republic

Venezuela is a federal presidential republic. The President serves as head of state and government, and a National Assembly enacts legislation, though recent political conflicts have affected governance.

Population

Ethnic Diversity

Venezuela has about 28 million residents, including mestizo, Indigenous, European, and Afro-Venezuelan communities. Spanish is the official language, with Indigenous languages spoken in rural regions.

Culture

Music and Dance

Venezuelan culture is rich in music and dance, including joropo, gaita, and salsa, reflecting Indigenous, African, and Spanish influences.

Arts and Festivals

Venezuela has a vibrant tradition of visual arts, literature, and folk festivals, particularly carnival celebrations in coastal regions.

Economy

Oil Dependence

Venezuela’s economy heavily depends on oil production, which accounts for the majority of exports and government revenue.

Other Sectors

Agriculture, mining, and services contribute to the economy, though political and economic challenges have affected stability.

Cuisine

Traditional Foods

Venezuelan cuisine includes arepas, pabellón criollo (rice, beans, meat, and plantains), hallacas, and empanadas, reflecting Indigenous, African, and Spanish influences.

Wildlife

Biodiversity Hotspots

Venezuela has jaguars, tapirs, capybaras, spectacled bears, monkeys, and hundreds of bird species. Its Amazonian and coastal ecosystems support rich aquatic life.

Tourism

Natural Attractions

Tourists visit Angel Falls, Canaima National Park, the Orinoco River, Los Roques archipelago, and Andean mountain ranges.

Cultural Tourism

Historic cities like Caracas and Mérida offer museums, colonial architecture, and cultural festivals.

Fun Facts

Angel Falls

Angel Falls, at 979 meters, is the world’s tallest uninterrupted waterfall.

Oil Reserves

Venezuela has some of the largest proven oil reserves in the world, mainly located in the Orinoco Belt.
