Geography

Island Paradise in the Indian Ocean

The Maldives is a tropical nation made up of around 1,190 coral islands grouped into 26 atolls, located southwest of India and Sri Lanka in the Indian Ocean. It is renowned for its low-lying islands and turquoise lagoons.

Coral Reefs and Lagoons

The islands are encircled by vibrant coral reefs that protect the land from erosion and host diverse marine ecosystems, making the Maldives one of the world’s most important coral reef regions.

History

Early Settlements

The Maldives has been inhabited for over 2,500 years, with early settlers arriving from South India and Sri Lanka, developing a culture based on fishing and seafaring.

Islamic Conversion and Sultans

Islam was introduced in the 12th century, transforming the islands into an Islamic sultanate that lasted for centuries, influencing governance, culture, and architecture.

Colonial Influence and Independence

The Maldives was a British protectorate from 1887 to 1965, when it gained full independence, later becoming a republic in 1968.

Government

Presidential Republic

The Maldives is a presidential republic. The President serves as both the head of state and government, elected by popular vote, and the unicameral parliament (People’s Majlis) oversees legislation.

Population

Small and Concentrated

The Maldives has around 550,000 residents, mostly concentrated on the capital island of Malé. The population is ethnically homogeneous, primarily of South Asian descent, and predominantly Muslim.

Culture

Islamic Traditions and Festivals

Islam deeply influences Maldivian culture. Religious observances like Ramadan and Eid are central to daily life, shaping social norms and celebrations.

Music and Dance

Traditional arts include boduberu drumming, dances, and folk songs, often performed during festivals and community gatherings.

Economy

Tourism-Driven Economy

Tourism is the backbone of the Maldivian economy, with luxury resorts, diving, and snorkeling attracting visitors worldwide.

Fishing Industry

Fishing, especially tuna, is another major sector, providing food, export revenue, and employment for locals.

Cuisine

Seafood Staples

Maldivian cuisine revolves around fish, particularly tuna, combined with coconut, rice, and curry spices. Dishes like mas huni (tuna with coconut) are eaten for breakfast and throughout the day.

Influences from the Region

Cuisine shows influences from India, Sri Lanka, and Arabia, using chili, curry leaves, and coconut milk in daily meals.

Wildlife

Marine Biodiversity

The Maldives is home to a rich array of marine life including manta rays, whale sharks, tropical fish, and vibrant coral species, making it a top destination for snorkeling and diving.

Birds and Sea Turtles

Seabirds like frigatebirds, terns, and noddies nest on remote islands, while green and hawksbill turtles are common in the surrounding waters.

Tourism

Luxury Resorts and Island Hopping

The Maldives is famous for overwater bungalows, pristine beaches, and private island resorts, attracting honeymooners and luxury travelers.

Diving and Water Sports

Coral reefs, clear waters, and abundant marine life make snorkeling, scuba diving, surfing, and other water sports highly popular.

Fun Facts

Lowest Country on Earth

The Maldives has the lowest average ground level of any country, with most islands rising only about 1.5 meters above sea level.

Island Nation with Hundreds of Islands

Although there are roughly 1,190 islands, only around 200 are inhabited, with the rest used for resorts or left as natural preserves.
