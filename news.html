What's new in the world

  • 2025
  • 2024
  • 2023
  • 2022

October 2025

New biometric border checks (fingerprints & photos) went into effect for non-EU travelers entering the Schengen area under the EU’s Entry-Exit System (EES). The change is being phased in over several months.

July 2025

The NBA announced that the Memphis Grizzlies and Orlando Magic would play two regular-season games in Europe: in Berlin (January 15, 2026) and London (January 18, 2026). The Berlin match will mark the first-ever regular-season NBA game in Germany.

March 2025

The UK economy unexpectedly shrank by 0.1 % in January, driven by a contraction in manufacturing and construction. This raised concerns about the near-term outlook as the government prepared its spring statement.

January 2025

Russia launched an overnight drone assault on Ukraine, sending at least 61 drones toward Ukrainian targets. Ukrainian defenses reportedly shot down 46 of them. Also in January: in Slovakia, protests erupted over Prime Minister Fico’s visit to Moscow and his government’s perceived pro-Russian stance. On January 24, tens of thousands marched in Bratislava, Košice and other cities, chanting slogans like “Slovakia is not Russia, Slovakia is Europe.”

December 2024

Syrian opposition forces took control of Damascus, forcing President Bashar al-Assad to flee and ending over a decade of his rule. The event marked a historic shift in Middle Eastern politics.

November 2024

The World Meteorological Organization confirmed 2024 as the hottest year on record, surpassing 1.5 °C above pre-industrial averages for the first time and raising alarm over global climate tipping points.

July 2024

Labour leader Keir Starmer won a landslide victory in the UK general election, ending 14 years of Conservative government in a dramatic political shift.

May 2024

Iranian President Ebrahim Raisi and Foreign Minister Hossein Amirabdollahian died in a helicopter crash in East Azerbaijan Province, triggering mourning and leadership changes in Tehran.

April 2024

A rare total solar eclipse crossed North America, with millions witnessing the moon completely obscure the sun from Mexico through the U.S. to Canada.

January 2024

A powerful magnitude 7.5 earthquake struck Japan’s Noto Peninsula, killing over 460 people and devastating communities across Ishikawa Prefecture.

December 2023

The United Nations COP28 summit concluded in Dubai with a landmark agreement to “transition away from fossil fuels,” though critics argued it lacked firm deadlines or enforcement.

October 2023

The Israel–Hamas conflict erupted after Hamas launched a surprise attack on southern Israel on October 7, killing over 1,000 civilians. The assault triggered a prolonged and devastating war in Gaza.

July 2023

Scientists confirmed July 2023 as the hottest month ever recorded globally, with record-breaking heatwaves across Europe, the U.S., and China.

June 2023

The Titan submersible imploded during a dive to the Titanic wreck, killing all five aboard, including OceanGate CEO Stockton Rush. The tragedy sparked debates over deep-sea tourism safety.

March 2023

Silicon Valley Bank collapsed, marking the largest U.S. bank failure since 2008 and sparking global financial market turbulence and emergency regulatory action.

January 2023

China officially reopened its borders after nearly three years of COVID-19 restrictions, signaling the end of its zero-COVID policy and the full reopening of the world’s second-largest economy.

December 2022

Argentina won the FIFA World Cup in Qatar after a thrilling final against France, decided by penalties. Lionel Messi lifted the trophy, capping one of the greatest football careers in history.

September 2022

Queen Elizabeth II, the longest-reigning monarch in British history, passed away at the age of 96. Her son Charles ascended the throne as King Charles III.

August 2022

U.S. Speaker Nancy Pelosi visited Taiwan, defying warnings from Beijing and escalating tensions between the United States and China over Taiwan’s sovereignty.

June 2022

The U.S. Supreme Court overturned Roe v. Wade, ending the federal right to abortion and triggering widespread protests and legal battles across the United States.

February 2022

Russia launched a full-scale invasion of Ukraine, marking the largest military conflict in Europe since World War II and triggering global sanctions and condemnation.

January 2022

A massive volcanic eruption near Tonga triggered tsunamis across the Pacific Ocean, disrupting communication lines and causing widespread damage to the island nation.
